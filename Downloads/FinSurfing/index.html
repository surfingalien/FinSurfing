Trading Strategies

Evidence-based strategies for US equities. Each strategy includes entry conditions, risk management, and key indicators.

Trend Following

Golden Cross / Death Cross

A classic long-term trend signal using the 50-day and 200-day simple moving averages. When the 50-SMA crosses above the 200-SMA, it signals a potential bull trend reversal.

Entry Conditions (Long)
  • 50-SMA crosses above 200-SMA (Golden Cross)
  • Price is above both moving averages
  • Volume confirmation on crossover day
  • RSI above 50 (trend confirmation)
Risk Management
  • Stop loss: below 200-SMA
  • Take profit: trailing stop at 50-SMA
  • Position size: 2-5% of portfolio
  • Exit: Death Cross (50-SMA below 200-SMA)

Mean Reversion

RSI Oversold/Overbought Reversal

Uses RSI to identify extreme momentum conditions. Stocks that are oversold (RSI <30) or overbought (RSI >70) tend to revert to the mean, creating short-term trading opportunities.

Entry Conditions (Long)
  • RSI drops below 30 (oversold zone)
  • RSI starts turning upward (confirmation)
  • Price at or near key support level
  • No strong downtrend in 200-SMA
Risk Management
  • Stop loss: 2–3% below entry
  • Take profit: RSI reaches 50–60
  • Avoid in strong downtrends
  • Best on higher time frames (daily+)
RSI(14) Support Levels SMA200
Momentum

MACD Momentum Crossover

MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) captures changes in momentum. The crossover of the MACD line above its signal line is a classic bullish entry signal.

Entry Conditions (Long)
  • MACD line crosses above Signal line
  • Crossover happens below zero line (stronger)
  • Histogram turns positive and expanding
  • Price in uptrend (above SMA50)
Risk Management
  • Stop loss: below recent swing low
  • Exit: MACD crosses back below signal
  • Avoid during high VIX / market stress
  • Works best in trending markets
MACD(12,26,9) SMA50 Volume
Breakout

Bollinger Band Squeeze

When Bollinger Bands narrow (squeeze), it signals a period of low volatility that often precedes a large directional move. Trading the breakout from the squeeze can be highly profitable.

Entry Conditions
  • BB Width at 6-month low (squeeze forming)
  • Price breaks above upper band (bullish)
  • Volume spike confirms breakout
  • MACD trending in breakout direction
Risk Management
  • Stop loss: back inside the bands
  • Target: 1.5x–2x the band width
  • False breakouts are common — wait for close
  • Ride with trailing stop after breakout
Bollinger Bands BB Width Volume MACD
Value / Fundamental

Value + Momentum Combo

Combines fundamental cheapness (low P/E, strong earnings) with technical momentum (price uptrend). Often called "GARP" — Growth At a Reasonable Price.

Selection Criteria
  • Forward P/E below sector average
  • EPS growth > 15% YoY
  • Revenue growth > 10% YoY
  • Profit margin expanding
  • Debt/Equity below 1.0
  • Price above 50-SMA and 200-SMA
Risk Management
  • Hold 3–12 months (position trade)
  • Diversify across 8–12 stocks
  • Rebalance quarterly
  • Exit if fundamentals deteriorate
P/E Ratio EPS Growth SMA50 SMA200
Relative Strength

Relative Strength (52-Week Leaders)

Stocks making new 52-week highs on strong volume tend to continue outperforming. This strategy exploits the momentum anomaly documented extensively in academic literature.

Entry Conditions
  • Stock within 5% of 52-week high
  • Outperforming S&P 500 over 3 months
  • Volume above 30-day average
  • RS Rating above 80 (IBD-style)
  • Base breakout from consolidation
Risk Management
  • Stop loss: 7–8% from entry (IBD rule)
  • Buy in tranches as stock moves up
  • Sell half at 20–25% gain
  • Exit if market goes into correction

Technical Analysis Guide

Master the indicators used in FinSurf and understand how to read market signals.

RSI

Relative Strength Index

RSI measures the speed and magnitude of recent price changes on a scale of 0–100. Developed by J. Welles Wilder in 1978.

RSI = 100 – (100 / (1 + RS))
RS = Avg Gain / Avg Loss over N periods
< 30 Oversold — potential buy
30–70 Neutral zone
> 70 Overbought — potential sell

Pro Tip: RSI divergence (price makes new high but RSI doesn't) is one of the strongest reversal signals in technical analysis.

MACD

Moving Average Convergence Divergence

MACD is a trend-following momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two exponential moving averages.

MACD = EMA(12) – EMA(26)
Signal = EMA(9) of MACD
Histogram = MACD – Signal
MACD > Signal Bullish momentum
MACD < Signal Bearish momentum
Histogram expanding Trend strengthening

Pro Tip: Bullish crossovers below the zero line (negative MACD turning positive) are often the strongest buy signals.

BB

Bollinger Bands

Created by John Bollinger, these bands use standard deviations to measure volatility and identify potential breakouts or reversals.

Upper = SMA(20) + 2σ
Middle = SMA(20)
Lower = SMA(20) – 2σ
Price near Lower Potential reversal up
Bands narrow Volatility squeeze — big move coming
Price near Upper Potential reversal down

Pro Tip: "Walking the bands" — when price hugs the upper band for many candles — signals a powerful uptrend, not a sell signal.

SMA

Simple Moving Averages

Moving averages smooth out price data and are the foundation of trend identification. The 50-day and 200-day SMAs are the most widely followed.

SMA(n) = Sum of closing prices / n
Price > SMA200 Long-term uptrend
Golden Cross SMA50 crosses above SMA200
Death Cross SMA50 crosses below SMA200

Pro Tip: Institutional investors (hedge funds, mutual funds) closely watch the 200-day SMA as a key support/resistance level, making it a self-fulfilling prophecy.

EMA

Exponential Moving Average

EMA gives more weight to recent prices, making it more responsive to new information than SMA. Popular choices: EMA(12), EMA(26), EMA(50).

EMA = Price × k + EMA(prev) × (1–k)
k = 2 / (n + 1)
Price > EMA(50) Short-term bullish
EMA(12) > EMA(26) MACD positive
Price < EMA(50) Short-term bearish

Pro Tip: Day traders use the 9 and 20 EMA for short-term signals, while swing traders prefer the 21 and 50 EMA.

VOL

Volume Analysis

Volume confirms price moves. A breakout on high volume is far more reliable than one on low volume. Volume precedes price in many cases.

Volume Ratio = Today's Volume / 30-Day Avg Volume
High Vol + Up Day Strong accumulation
Low Vol + Down Day Normal pullback, not panic
High Vol + Down Day Distribution / selling pressure

Pro Tip: "Climax volume" — an extreme spike in volume on a big price move — often marks the end of a trend rather than the continuation.

Trading Glossary
Support
A price level where buying pressure is expected to prevent further decline
Resistance
A price level where selling pressure is expected to prevent further rise
Beta
Measures a stock's volatility relative to the market. Beta > 1 = more volatile
P/E Ratio
Price-to-Earnings ratio. Compares stock price to annual earnings per share
EPS
Earnings Per Share — company's profit divided by outstanding shares
Float
The number of shares available for public trading (not held by insiders)
Short Interest
Percentage of float that has been sold short (borrowed and sold)
52W Range
The high and low price over the past 52 weeks — a key volatility measure
VWAP
Volume-Weighted Average Price — intraday benchmark used by institutions
ATR
Average True Range — measures daily volatility; used to set stop losses
Divergence
When price and an indicator (RSI/MACD) move in opposite directions — powerful reversal signal
Doji
Candlestick pattern with small body indicating market indecision
